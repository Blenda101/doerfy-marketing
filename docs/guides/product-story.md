# Doerfy: The Complete Product Story

**Purpose:** This document tells the complete story of the Doerfy product suite — what it is, why it exists, how the methodology connects to every tool, and what each feature does. Written for agents and team members who need an accurate, complete mental model of the product before working in it.

**Status markers used throughout:**
- ✅ **Live** — shipped and running in production
- 🔄 **In progress** — actively being built
- 📋 **Handoff ready** — spec complete, not yet built
- 🗺️ **Roadmap** — direction set, specification pending

## Part 1: The Story

Doerfy exists because most productivity tools solve the wrong problem.

You can be perfectly efficient at the wrong life. You can capture every thought, organize every task, and ship every project — and still end a year feeling like you spent the hours on someone else's agenda. The tools got better; the problem didn't.

The Doerfy Method is built on a different premise: **before you can do the right work, you have to design the right life.** The framework has three phases that move in sequence — Design, Visualize, Do — and one principle that runs through all of them: a life worth living must be *authored*, not defaulted into.

### Two Perspectives on Doerfy

Doerfy is best understood through two lenses that operate independently but reinforce each other:

**Perspective 1 — The Core Framework: Design · Visualize · Do**
The top-level methodology. Three phases in sequence that take a Doer from life design to daily execution. This is the *what* of the Method.

**Perspective 2 — The Second Brain and its OS**
A separate dimension. The Second Brain has two layers: an **Execution layer** (Stories, Todo, Tasks) and a **Knowledge layer** (Notes, Library, Canvas, Post). The Improvement Space is its operating system — the continuous loop that keeps the second brain from becoming passive storage. This is the *how* of the Method.

These two perspectives are not nested inside each other. The OS (Improvement Space) is central to *all three phases* of the Core Framework — it is present in Design, in Visualize, and in Do, operating differently at each phase. The Second Brain feeds and is fed by all three phases.

**Do not conflate them.** The Core Framework explains the Doer's journey. The Second Brain + OS explains how knowledge flows and compounds throughout that journey.

### The Three Phases (Core Framework)

**Design** is where the life becomes intentional. With Doey, the persistent AI coach who lives across every space in the product, the Doer works through the Four ACTs — Vision, Mission, Values, Strategy — using a Socratic conversation grounded in decades of coaching tradition: GROW, Ikigai, ACT, SMART, OKR. The output is a written Script: the master story of the life the Doer intends to build.

**Visualize** is where the designed life becomes seeable, and is kept visible. The Script is rendered into Storyboards and Movies the Doer can watch. Goals become instrumented cards. The Outcome Pyramid is a graph. The Action Funnel is a shape. Visualization is not a feature — it is a phase of the method and a quality that runs through every screen.

**Do** is where the designed life is executed. The Theme decomposes into Mega Dos, Projects, Todos, and Tasks. Work flows through the Action Funnel — from the unlimited Do Queue down to a single Task being worked on right now.

This is not a task manager with goal-setting bolted on. **The framework is the product.**

Three failures the Method directly addresses:

- **The vision-execution gap:** Most productivity apps live downstream of the gap — they help you do *something* without ever asking whether the something is the right something. Doerfy starts with Vision first.
- **The capture-overwhelm spiral:** Capture without Discernment is just a cluttered inbox. The Improvement Space's COP process (Clarify, Organize, Prioritize) turns captures into committed action.
- **The planning-paralysis loop:** The Elevation Framework keeps planning brief and cadenced. Most time is spent at ground level, doing. Planning happens on schedule; execution is the default mode.

### The Second Brain

The Second Brain is the Doer's complete personal operating environment — everything except the Theme/Design layer. It has two layers:

**Execution layer (top)** — where the second brain drives action:
- **Stories** (Planning → Outcomes) — your *what & why*. The Outcome Pyramid made operational.
- **Tasks** (Work → Actions) — your *how & when*. The Action Funnel in motion.
- **Todo** — the bridge at center. Connects Outcomes to Actions. The architectural hinge between planning and execution.

**Knowledge layer (bottom)** — where the second brain captures and creates:
- **Capture mode** (Notes, Library) — curate what comes in: ideas, links, files, references
- **Creation mode** (Canvas, Post) — produce what goes out: visual thinking, published writing

The second brain is not passive storage. The execution layer drives what gets done. The knowledge layer feeds what gets thought. Doey connects everything — ingesting captures, surfacing connections to active Goals and Mega Dos, and ensuring the knowledge layer compounds into the execution layer.

The Improvement Space is the **OS of the second brain** — the system that ensures captures flow through COP into committed work, the execution nodes stay honest, and the Doer continuously gets better at doing.

### The Improvement Space: The OS (CIM)

The Improvement Space is the CIM — the **Continuous Improvement Model** — and it is the operating system: central to all three phases. It does not live only inside "Do." It runs continuously across Design, Visualize, and Do, operating at different intensities in each.

**The Doer's relationship to the CIM is a practice, not a workflow.** The CIM is not a checklist to complete — it is a discipline to internalize. The Doer who has truly embedded the CIM does not consult the model; they *live* it. Capture is reflexive. COP is habitual. Commit is clear. Plan/Review/Do is rhythmic. This is the goal the Method is designed to produce.

**How the OS operates in each phase:**

| Phase | OS role | Primary activities |
|---|---|---|
| **Design** | Mostly planning; capturing and COP-ing ideas into Theme and Stories | Capture new ideas for Vision/Mission/Values/Strategy; COP them into the design; commit the ones that belong in Outcome Pyramid |
| **Visualize** | Generating and reviewing visual outputs | Wheel ratings, Storyboard renders, Movie generation, Goals check-ins, Reports and data visualization, Knowledge connections |
| **Do** | Full operational mode — the OS running at 100% | Capture (always on) → COP → Commit → Do (70%) + Review (20%) + Plan (10%) |

### The Action Funnel (Act)

```
┌─────────────────────────────────┐  ∞    Todo Queue   Active Todos waiting for task breakdown
├─────────────────────────────────┤  ∞    Do Queue     Tasks ready for prioritization
├─────────────────────────────────┤  30   Do(30)       Committed for the next ~30 days
├─────────────────────────────────┤   7   Doing(7)     Committed for this week
├─────────────────────────────────┤   1   Do Today(1)  Today's single most important task
├─────────────────────────────────┤   1   Do Now       The task currently being worked on
└─────────────────────────────────┘  ∞    Done         Completed tasks + Doey pattern analysis
```

The width caps are the method's commitment discipline: narrowing from unlimited to one forces the Doer to choose what actually gets done.

### The Improvement Space — The OS Diagram

```
 ┌──────────────────┬──────────────────────────┬───────────────────────────┐
 │                  │                          │  Plan              ← 10k  │
 │                  │   Clarify,               ├───────────────────────────┤
 │                  │   Organize &             │  Review            ← 5k   │
 │    Capture       │   Prioritize             ├───────────────────────────┤
 │                  │                          │  Do                ← 0k   │
 │                  ├──────────────────────────┤                           │
 │                  │  Commit  ↑0%  ↓100%      │                           │
 └──────────────────┴──────────────────────────┴───────────────────────────┘
   Always active       Process + Gate              Elevations
```

**Continuously Get Better at Doing.**

**Capture** (left column, full height) — always active. Get things out of your head immediately. No filtering, no judging. The discipline is zero friction: ideas, tasks, observations all land here at the moment of conception.

**COP: Clarify · Organize · Prioritize** (middle column, upper) — process what's been captured:
- **Clarify** — *What is it?* What exactly is this item and what would done look like?
- **Organize** — *Where should it go?* Route it to the right part of the system.
- **Prioritize** — *Relevance in relation to others?* How important is this against everything already committed?

**Commit** (middle column, lower) — the WIP gate. Not a binary switch — a 0–100% value that routes items to the correct funnel stage:
- 100% → Do Now / Do Today(1)
- 70%+ → Doing(7)
- 40%+ → Do(30)
- 0–40% → Do Queue

**Plan · Review · Do** (right column, three elevations):
- **Plan** (10k ft) — quarterly/annual, strategic perspective
- **Review** (5k ft) — weekly/monthly, tactical perspective
- **Do** (0k ft) — daily, immediate execution

**Three valid paths to Commit:**
```
  Capture ──────────────────────────────────────────────→ Commit
  (direct — you capture something and immediately know it's committed WIP)

  Capture → COP → Commit
  (standard — capture, then clarify/organize/prioritize, then commit)

  Plan | Review → COP → Commit
  (altitude work surfaces new priorities; they go through COP before committing at 0k)
```

**Commit is not the end of a sequence — it is a gate every input eventually reaches.** Reviewing at 10k ft and 5k ft surfaces items that need COP processing before being committed at ground level.

**OS time allocation by cadence:**

| Rhythm | Do | Review | Plan |
|---|---|---|---|
| Daily | 80% | 10% | 10% |
| Weekly | 60% | 20% | 20% |
| Monthly | 50% | 30% | 20% |
| Annual | 10% | 30% | 60% |

**CSD** (Capture · Stage · Deliver) is the operational vocabulary — used in UI, onboarding, and day-to-day reference.
**CDC** (Conception · Discernment · Cadence) is the philosophical vocabulary — used in the Manifesto, coaching, and public essays. Both name the same activities; context determines which to use.

### Tool 1: Theme Builder ✅ Live

**What it is:** The top-level authoring environment where the Doer designs their life (or business) as a master story — the Theme.

**Method role:** Design phase (authoring the Script) + Visualize phase (Story and Goals tabs). The apex of the Outcome Pyramid.

**Three tabs:**

#### Script tab
Where the Four ACTs are authored through Doey's Socratic coaching:

- **Profile** — structured facts about who the Doer is: context, working style, constraints. Injected into every Doey conversation at every level of the product as universal coaching context.
- **Wheel of Life** (Personal) / **Wheel of Focus** (Business) — rate each life/focus area 1–10 for satisfaction, then rank by importance. Combined Rate + Rank produces the **Priority Score** — surfaces the most under-served areas. Runs at the Monthly Tune-Up cadence.
- **Act I — Vision** — the 5–10 year picture of the life or business being built. Evocative, not yet specific outcomes.
- **Act II — Mission** — the 3–5 year campaign toward the Vision. Time-bound and completable (more like a NASA mission than a corporate mission statement). Refreshed annually.
- **Act III — Values** — the Doer's personal non-negotiables. Distinct from the Doer Manifesto (which is the Method's creed). Each Value is paired with an action sentence: what honoring it looks like in practice.
- **Act IV — Strategy** — the load-bearing ACT. The keystone that connects Theme inputs (Vision, Mission, Values) to Theme outputs (Goals, first Mega Dos). Answers: *"Given my Vision, Mission, and Values — how will I specifically execute this year?"* Includes team composition as a core element. Refreshed at each Quarterly Reset.

**Strategy generates three parallel streams (not hierarchically nested):**
- Goals (achievement, habit, metric, or elimination shapes)
- Habits (recurring practices)
- First Mega Dos (identity-level outcomes for the Outcome Pyramid)

#### Story tab
Where the Theme is rendered into visual artifacts:

- **Storyboards** — frame-by-frame visual rendering of the Script (panels combining images, overlays, and text)
- **Movies** — Storyboard animated with Ken Burns-style motion and AI-generated voiceover. The Doer watches a trailer for their own life.
- **Banners** — single hero-image visual reminders on the home screen and at the top of Theme-related views

#### Goals tab
Where Strategy outputs become instrumented, trackable commitments:

- **Goals & Habits** (Personal Workspace / Life Coach mode) — all using SMART architecture
  - Achievement Goals: discrete one-time outcomes
  - Habit Goals: recurring practices
  - Metric Goals: quantitative thresholds
  - Elimination Goals: things to stop or reduce
- **OKRs** (Organization / Business Coach mode) — standard objective-and-key-results format

Data architecture: Goals and Habits share a single `goals` table with `is_habit: true/false`. Operationally distinct, data-model unified.

**Workspace types:**
- **Personal Workspace** — Life Theme, Life Coach Doey, Wheel of Life, Goals & Habits. Tiers: Personal ($5.99/mo), Info Worker ($9.99/mo).
- **Organization** — Business Theme, Business Coach Doey, Wheel of Focus, OKRs. Tiers: Pro ($19.99/mo), Team ($12.99/user/mo).

A single Doer can have both workspaces simultaneously.

### Tool 2: Stories (Outcome Space) ✅ Live

**What it is:** The visual planning environment where the Theme is decomposed into a nested hierarchy of executable outcomes — the Outcome Pyramid in practice.

**Method role:** Outcome Space (the Plan half of the Do phase). Holds **Outcome Planning Stories** — distinct from Theme Visual Stories (Storyboards/Movies).

**The four-tier hierarchy:**

| Tier | Name | What it is | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| Apex | Theme | The master story | "Build a life of creative impact and financial freedom" |
| Level 2 | Mega Do | Identity-level outcome; who you're *becoming* | "Become a consistently producing writer" |
| Level 3 | Project | Shippable deliverable with a clear done | "Launch personal essay newsletter" |
| Level 4 | Todo | Work stream within a project | "Build subscriber list", "Write first 10 essays" |

**Key distinctions that trip people up:**
- A Mega Do is not shipped — it is *become*. ("Become someone for whom health is default," not "Lose 20 pounds.")
- A Project ships; a Mega Do compounds.
- A Todo is a work stream (outcome statement); a Task is an atomic action (execution unit).

**Fields on every Outcome Story:**
- **Story field** — narrative prose. *Why* this matters.
- **What Does Done Look Like (WDDLL)** — the success state. Agile user story format: *"As [role], I want [outcome] so that [benefit]."* How we'll know when it's done.

**AI coaching architecture (Two-Tier Coach Pattern):**
- **Overall Story Coach** (module-level) — full Theme context; shares conversationId with Theme Builder for seamless continuity; can generate full cascades (propose Mega Dos → user approves → items created in UI)
- **Individual Outcome Coach** (item-level) — scoped to one Mega Do, Project, or Todo; parent chain + sibling context only
- **Coaching posture by level:** Mega Do Coach = Strategic Initiative Architect; Project Coach = Deliverable Shaper; Todo Coach = Execution-Ready Outcome Surfacer

**Sibling Awareness:** each coach knows the other items at the same level (other Projects under this Mega Do, other Todos under this Project). Prevents duplicate work and scope creep.

**Generation Cascade UX:** Suggest (chat proposal) → Approve (user confirms in chat) → Create (tool calls execute, items appear in UI with "newly created" indicator). Never auto-creates without approval.

**Outcome-to-Action Bridge:** Setting a Todo's status to **Active** surfaces it in the Todo Queue of the Action Funnel. The Todo itself does not move into the funnel — only its status changes. This is the architectural hinge connecting planning to execution.

**Direct Entry:** Tasks and Todos can be added directly to the Action Funnel without pyramid parentage. Quick errands, ad-hoc requests, and one-off work don't need ceremony. The aggregate of what a Doer does still reflects how they are living the Theme, even without formal connections.

### Tool 3: Tasks (Action Space) ✅ Live

**What it is:** The execution environment where committed Tasks flow through the six-stage Action Funnel, from queue to done.

**Method role:** Action Space (the Act half of the Do phase).

**The Action Funnel in detail:**

| Stage | Width cap | Commitment level | What lives here |
|---|---|---|---|
| Todo Queue | Unlimited | Active Todos ready for task breakdown | Todos with Active status |
| Do Queue | Unlimited | Tasks ready to prioritize | All uncommitted tasks |
| Do(30) | 30 | This month's committed tasks | Tasks to ship in ~30 days |
| Doing(7) | 7 | This week's committed tasks | Reset every Weekly Review |
| Do Today(1) | 1 | Today's single task | Set every morning |
| Do Now | 1 | Currently being worked on | Set by the Doer in the moment |
| Done | Unlimited | Completed tasks | Doey runs pattern analysis here |

**Three task views:**
- **Stage view** (Action Funnel) — the primary time-boxing view; the method's default
- **List view** — flat sortable list with filters and property editing
- **Calendar view** — tasks mapped to time blocks on a calendar

**Task properties:** priority, energy level, location, due date, time estimate, parent project/todo, labels

**Task aging:** visual indicators when tasks sit in a stage too long without progress. Doey surfaces avoidance patterns (tasks moved 3+ times without completion) during Daily check-ins.

**Work tracking:** time entries logged per task. Supports the Done Reporting module and Doey's weekly pattern analysis.

### Tool 4: The Improvement Space — The OS (CIM) ✅ Live (Capture & Stage surfaces); 🔄 In Progress (ritual UI)

**What it is:** The CIM — the **Continuous Improvement Model** — functioning as the operating system of Doerfy. Central to all three phases (Design, Visualize, Do). The continuous loop that turns captures into committed work and makes the Doer continuously better at doing.

**Method role:** The OS runs *across* all phases, not inside any single one. In Design, it captures and COP-s ideas into Theme and Stories. In Visualize, it surfaces data for the Wheel, Goals, and reports. In Do, it runs at full intensity — Capture + COP + Commit + Do/Review/Plan.

**The Doer's job with the CIM is to practice it until it is embedded** — not to use it as a checklist but to internalize it as a way of moving through work. Doey is the agent in this loop: surfacing the right ritual at the right time, noting patterns, and asking the questions that keep the practice alive.

**The Doer is always in capture mode.** Capture is not a phase that turns on and off — it is a persistent posture. The system is always receiving.

**The three operations:**

**Capture** — get things out of your head the moment they appear. No filtering, no judging, no organizing. Ideas, tasks, requirements, observations — all land in the Capture surface immediately at the moment of conception. The mind is for designing; the system holds what the mind cannot carry.

**COP (Clarify · Organize · Prioritize)** — process raw captures into something actionable:
- **Clarify** — *What is it?* Define exactly what the item is and what done looks like.
- **Organize** — *Where does it belong?* Route it to the right part of the system with the right connections.
- **Prioritize** — *Relevance in relation to others?* Decide its importance against everything else already committed.

**Commit** — the gate that routes processed items into Work In Progress (WIP). The Commit gate is a 0–100% commitment value, not a binary switch:
- 100% → Do Now / Do Today(1) — being worked on now or today
- 70%+ → Doing(7) — committed this week
- 40%+ → Do(30) — committed this month
- 0–40% → Do Queue — on deck but not yet committed

**Three valid paths to Commit:**

1. **Capture → Commit directly** — you capture something and immediately know it's committed WIP (e.g., a task someone just handed you that starts now)
2. **Capture → COP → Commit** — the standard flow: capture first, then clarify/organize/prioritize, then commit
3. **Plan | Review → COP → Commit** — reviewing at altitude (10k ft / 5k ft) surfaces new priorities; those pass through COP before being committed at ground level

**Deliver** — execution across three elevations of the Elevation Framework:

| Altitude | Name | Time horizon | Primary entities | Focus |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 10,000 ft | Plan | Quarterly to annual | Theme, Mega Dos, Strategy, OKRs | Strategic perspective |
| 5,000 ft | Review | Weekly to monthly | Projects, Do(30), Wheel | Tactical perspective |
| 0 ft | Do | Daily to this week | Doing(7), Do Today(1), Tasks | Immediate execution |

**Time allocation by cadence rhythm:**

| Rhythm | Do | Review | Plan |
|---|---|---|---|
| Daily | 80% | 10% | 10% |
| Weekly | 60% | 20% | 20% |
| Monthly | 50% | 30% | 20% |
| Annual | 10% | 30% | 60% |

**Method role:** Strategy outputs from Act IV, surfaced in the Theme Builder's Goals tab and tracked throughout the Doerfy Cadence. Cross-referenced to Outcome Planning Stories via Labels.

**Two modes by workspace type:**

**Goals & Habits** (Personal Workspace):
- Achievement Goals — discrete one-time outcomes ("Run a marathon by December")
- Habit Goals — recurring practices to establish ("Meditate every morning")
- Metric Goals — quantitative thresholds ("Read 24 books this year")
- Elimination Goals — things to stop or reduce ("Reduce social media to under 30 min/day")

**OKRs** (Organization):
- Objectives: qualitative, inspiring statements of what to achieve
- Key Results: measurable outcomes that define what success looks like (typically 3–5 per objective)

**Data architecture:** Goals and Habits share a single `goals` table with `is_habit: true/false`. Operationally distinct; data-model unified.

**Features:** visual goal cards, progress bars, goal check-ins at cadence, habit streak counting, achievement tracking, cross-reference to Outcome Planning Stories via Labels field.

### Tool 10: Doey (The Coach) ✅ Live

**What it is:** The persistent AI coach — one identity, one memory, one coaching relationship running through every space in Doerfy. To the Doer, Doey is a coach: warm, specific, present at every layer. To the system, Doey is the AI agent in the CIM loop.

**Method role:** Agent in the CIM loop. Doey has two purposes, in order:

1. **Primary — embed the practice.** Help the Doer become the kind of person for whom the CIM is second nature. Capture is reflexive. COP is habitual. Commit is clear. Plan/Review/Do is rhythmic. Doey surfaces the right ritual, asks the right question, and notices what the Doer cannot see about their own patterns — all in service of making the practice stick.
2. **Secondary — do the work.** Generate cascades, draft content, decompose projects, summarize captures. Doey can act on behalf of the Doer — but this is always in service of the primary goal, not a replacement for it.

**The shared goal of Doer and Doey:** Embed the practice of getting life done. The Doer brings intention; Doey brings continuity, pattern recognition, and the right question at the right moment. Together they make the CIM a lived practice rather than a productivity system the Doer has to consciously manage.

Doey is not a feature. Doey is the connective tissue that turns tools into a practice and a practice into a life.

**Two coach modes:**
- **Life Coach** (Personal Workspace) — wide aperture; personal life topics, career, purpose work; SMART goal architecture; Ikigai when relevant for purpose discovery
- **Business Coach** (Organization) — narrow aperture; business assumed to be the Theme; OKR architecture; strategic positioning language

**Three coaching styles (selectable per Theme):**
- **Conversational** (default) — warm but direct, brief sentences, no exclamation points, no emoji
- **Professional** — formal register, fewer contractions, more declarative observations
- **Inspirational** — elevated at milestones, sparing purposeful emoji (max 1 per response), Manifesto and mantra invocation

**The Two-Tier Coach Pattern — universal architecture:**

| Tier | Name | Scope | Can do |
|---|---|---|---|
| Module Coach | Overall Story Coach, Goals Module Coach | Full module width | Generate cascades, orchestrate across items |
| Item Coach | Individual Outcome Coach, Individual Goal Coach | One item | Generate that item's direct children only |

Same Doey, different scope. Every module in the product follows this pattern (except Canvas).

**Coaching posture by Outcome level:**
- Mega Do Coach = Strategic Initiative Architect (long focal length, identity-level thinking)
- Project Coach = Deliverable Shaper (mid focal length, scope and delivery thinking)
- Todo Coach = Execution-Ready Outcome Surfacer (short focal length, the last coaching step before action)

**Memory architecture:**
- Per-item `conversationId` scoping prevents context bleed between unrelated items
- Overall Story Coach shares the Theme Builder's `conversationId` — coaching continuity across sessions
- Children do NOT inherit parent transcripts — memory flows downstream via parent's Story + WDDLL fields
- Life Profile injected at every level as universal coaching context
- `themeSummary` cached on the Theme record (~3 sentences) — used by lower-level coaches to receive distilled Theme context without raw ACT verbosity

**Framework knowledge:** Doey can retrieve methodology documents via RAG. Category-tagged: `consumer`, `business`, `shared`. Filtered based on coach mode.

### Tool 11: Doer Diagnostic ✅ Live

**What it is:** The signature ten-question assessment that opens the Method and routes each Doer to their correct starting point.

**Method role:** The front door to onboarding. Run quarterly as part of the Reset cadence. Available as a standalone public tool at doerfy.com/diagnostic.

**Structure:** 10 questions across 5 layers (2 per layer), answered on a 1–5 scale. Lowest-scoring layer determines the Doer Type.

| Layer | Q1 | Q2 |
|---|---|---|
| Theme | "I can describe the life I'm building over the next 5–10 years." | "I know what I'm trying to accomplish in the next 12 months — specifically enough to recognize success." |
| Outcome | "Most of what I'm working on connects clearly to a larger goal I've defined." | "For the next 90 days, I know the 3–5 major initiatives and what would make each one done." |
| Action | "On a typical day, I'm clear about what to work on next without re-deciding." | "I finish what I start more often than I abandon it." |
| Improve | "I have a regular practice (weekly or more) of reviewing what I've done and what's coming." | "I notice patterns in my work — what I'm avoiding, what energizes me, when I'm most productive." |
| Knowledge | "When I have an idea or insight, I capture it somewhere I'll actually return to." | "My notes and references genuinely help me think and produce." |

**Six Doer Types:**

| Type | Weakest layer | What's true | Routes to |
|---|---|---|---|
| Wandering Doer | Theme | Moving, but toward what? | Theme Builder → Act I (Vision) |
| Drifting Doer | Outcome | Has a vision but no structured initiatives | Stories → Mega Do creation |
| Scattered Doer | Action | Plans but execution is chaotic; too many open items | Tasks → Doing(7) triage |
| Reactive Doer | Improve | Producing but not learning; no review practice | Improvement Space → Weekly Review setup |
| Hollow Doer | Knowledge | Working system, empty mind; not compounding thinking | Knowledge Base → Notes/Library setup |
| Whole Doer | None | All layers strong; maintenance mode | Refinement experience |

**Tie-break rule:** if two layers tie, the higher layer in the methodology wins (Theme > Outcome > Action > Improve > Knowledge). Reasoning: downstream layers can't be fixed while upstream ones are broken.

**Output artifact:** Doer Type card with layer scores visualized as a five-segment bar chart, starting point recommendation, progression view (what comes next after fixing the weakest layer), and a shareable branded card.

**Marketing note:** The Diagnostic is designed as a standalone top-of-funnel tool. No account required to take it. Signup happens after the user sees their Type and wants to act on it.

## Part 5: The Five Cadence Rhythms

The Method runs on five nested rhythms. Without cadence, the Method is a one-time exercise. With it, the Method is a practice — and practices compound.

| Rhythm | Cadence | Duration | Altitude | What it produces |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **The Daily** | Every day | 5 min AM + 3 min PM | 0 ft (ground) | Clean Do Today(1) and closed day |
| **The Review** | Weekly | 30–45 min | 5k ft | Reset Doing(7), Done audit, Mega Do check, Theme glance, one captured reflection |
| **The Tune-Up** | Monthly | 60–90 min | Mid-high | Refreshed Wheel, updated Mega Dos, tracked Goals, one committed monthly change |
| **The Reset** | Quarterly | 2–3 hours | 10k ft | Fresh Diagnostic, Mission read, Strategy refresh, Mega Do harvest, three priority Mega Dos for the quarter |
| **The Re-Authoring** | Annually | Full day | Stratosphere | Re-authored Theme, new Script, refreshed Story, new Mission, new Goals, one annual sentence |

Each rhythm is Doey-led with defined structure and defined outputs. The nesting:

```
   ANNUAL — Theme re-authored. Sets the year.
     └── QUARTERLY — Mega Dos and Strategy refresh. Sets the 90 days.
           └── MONTHLY — Wheel, Goals, Knowledge tune. Sets the month.
                 └── WEEKLY — Doing(7) reset, Done audit. Sets the week.
                       └── DAILY — Do Today, Do Now. Sets the moment.
```

The Annual's Mission shapes the Quarterly's Mega Dos. The Quarterly's Strategy shapes the Monthly's Tune-Up. The Monthly's commitment shapes the Weekly's Doing(7). The Weekly's choices shape the Daily's Do Now.

## Part 6: The User Journey (Onboarding to Practicing Doer)

### Session 1 (~30 minutes)

```
 Phase 1 — ARRIVAL (3 min)
 Sign in → Doey introduces itself → Take the Doer Diagnostic → See Doer Type
 ↓
 Phase 2 — ORIENT (2 min)
 View master diagram → Choose Personal or Business → First workspace created
 ↓
 Phase 3 — DESIGN (15 min)
 Rate Wheel of Life/Focus → Doey interprets Priority Scores → Draft Vision (Act I) with Doey
 ↓
 Phase 4 — COMMIT (8 min)
 Create first Mega Do → Break into a Project → Name 2–4 Todos → Commit 1–3 Tasks to Doing(7)
 ↓
 Phase 5 — RHYTHM (2 min)
 Schedule first Weekly Review (calendar event) → Configure Daily check-in reminder
```

**What the Doer has by end of Session 1:** Doer Type, Theme container, Wheel rated and ranked with Priority Scores, Vision draft (Act I), one Mega Do, one Project with Todos, 1–3 Tasks in Doing(7), first Weekly Review scheduled, Daily rhythm configured.

### Days 1–7

- Daily check-ins with Doey (morning: set Do Today; evening: close the day)
- Mission (Act II) drafted on Day 3 prompt
- Values (Act III) drafted on Day 5 prompt
- First Weekly Review attended on Day 7 — full ritual runs with Doey leading
- First Story rendered after Script has more complete ACTs

### Days 7–30

- First Monthly Tune-Up
- Wheel re-rated
- Pyramid expanded to 2–3 Mega Dos
- First Task moved to Done and confirmed

### Day 90+

- First Quarterly Reset completed
- Strategy refreshed; three priority Mega Dos chosen for the quarter
- The user is a **practicing Doer**

**The activation funnel defined in artifacts, not events:**

| Timeframe | Milestone |
|---|---|
| End of Session 1 | Doer Type, Theme, Wheel, Vision, Mega Do, Project, Doing(7), scheduled Review |
| End of Day 7 | Four ACTs drafted, Story rendered, first Weekly Review attended, first Task Done |
| End of Day 30 | First Monthly Tune-Up, Wheel re-rated, pyramid expanded |
| End of Day 90 | First Quarterly Reset, practicing Doer |

## Part 8: What Makes Doerfy Different

**1. The framework is the product.**
Other tools give a blank page or an opinionated workflow that goes nowhere. Every Doerfy screen serves a phase. Every artifact has a defined role. The structure does the heavy lifting so the Doer can focus on the doing.

**2. The coach is persistent.**
Doey holds the Vision from Design time when helping prioritize Tasks at execution time. One memory. One coach. The Doer never has to re-explain themselves. No other productivity tool has this.

**3. Visualization is foundational, not decorative.**
The Theme is a movie. Goals are visual cards. The Outcome Pyramid is a graph. The Action Funnel is a shape. Doing is *seen*, not just done. Visualization is a phase, a quality, and a discipline — not a chart feature.

**4. The hierarchy is honest.**
Theme ≠ Mega Do ≠ Project ≠ Todo ≠ Task. Each has its own name, its own home, its own relationship to everything else. Most tools collapse these or confuse them. Doerfy names each precisely; users never wonder where something belongs.

**5. It works for a whole life or a whole business.**
Same architecture (Theme → Mega Do → Project → Todo → Task), same coach, different vocabulary: Life Coach vs. Business Coach, Wheel of Life vs. Wheel of Focus, Goals & Habits vs. OKRs. Learn the Method once; apply it across every domain.

**6. The methodology integrates the coaching canon.**
GROW, Ikigai, ACT, SMART, OKR are all present — not as competing frameworks but as integrated sources. Doerfy is not the inventor of coaching; it is the bridge between coaching tradition and daily action that the field had been missing.

**7. The cadence is built in.**
Methods without rhythm are one-time exercises. Doerfy ships five nested rhythms with Doey-led rituals. Users don't invent their practice; they enter it.

**8. The goal is practice, not productivity.**
Most tools optimize for throughput. Doerfy optimizes for the Doer becoming someone for whom doing the right things is natural. The CIM is not a workflow to use — it is a discipline to internalize. Doey's job is not to be useful; it is to make the Doer better. AI-centric competitors that outsource thinking cannot occupy this position structurally — it requires the human to remain the one in practice.

## Part 9: Key Relationships to Keep Straight

These distinctions generate the most confusion in development and coaching:

**Theme Visual Story vs. Outcome Planning Story**
— Theme Visual Story = Storyboards, banners, Movies (visualization of the Theme)
— Outcome Planning Story = Mega Do / Project / Todo (the execution hierarchy)
Both are called "Stories" but they serve different layers and live in different surfaces.

**Goals as Strategy output vs. Goals as an ACT**
— As of v3.0, Goals are *outputs* of Strategy (Act IV), not an ACT themselves.
— The Four ACTs are Vision, Mission, Values, Strategy. Goals flow from Strategy.

**Todo vs. Task**
— A Todo is a *work stream* (outcome statement). It plans.
— A Task is an *atomic action* (execution unit). It executes.
— Todos live in the Outcome Pyramid; Tasks live in the Action Funnel.
— The bridge: Todo with Active status generates Tasks.

**Mission vs. Vision**
— Vision is 5–10 years (destination). Mission is 3–5 years (direction toward that destination).

**Doer Manifesto vs. Values**
— The Doer Manifesto is the *Method's* ten principles (fixed for everyone).
— Values (Act III) are *the Doer's own* non-negotiables (unique per user).

**Doey vs. Doerfy**
— Doey is the AI coach. Doerfy is the platform. Never interchangeable.

**Library vs. Content**
— Same module. "Content" is the legacy name. "Library" is the canonical name as of v3.0.
